History

House Sparrows historically lived in Europe, but during the colonization of America were brought to America by settlers
who were homesick, where they rapidly spread across the entirety of the US. With globalization, they spread even further across the world.

General Appearance and Anatomy

House Sparrows are small and brown, with speckles of chestnut, grey, white and black creating a brownish gestalt. Males have a small black throat patch and grey patch on the top of their head, while females lack those distinguishing
features and are the 'base' House Sparrow.

Fun Facts

  • They are one of three species not protected federally.
  • They can swim if needs must.
  • Scientists have listened to radio broadcasts worldwide in order to determine their ever-expanding range
  • They can live up to 13 years.
